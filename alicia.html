The goal

Not more content.
Better content with a purpose.

The goal isn't to become a viral creator. It's to build content that genuinely helps Christian wives, earns their trust over time, and naturally leads the right women toward Alicia's coaching — the Six Intimacy Skills™. Everything in this plan is built around that single idea.

"Someone doesn't watch one Reel and book a coaching call. They discover Alicia, watch a few more videos, find her YouTube, and over weeks they begin to think — I'd love her to coach me. The content isn't selling coaching. The content is building the relationship that makes coaching the natural next step."

The problem we're solving

The current model
isn't sustainable.

Setting up a tripod multiple times a day to film separate 60-second videos is exhausting — and it produces content without a system behind it. One focused filming session per week with a clear plan replaces all of that. Less work, better quality, more consistency — and every piece of content connects back to Alicia's message and the women she serves.

Thumbnail strategy

The grid is a storefront.
Right now it's not selling.

When someone discovers Alicia through a clip and visits her profile, the grid is the first thing they see. It needs to instantly communicate: this is a real person, she knows what she's talking about, and I want to watch more. Right now it isn't doing that — and it's costing her followers every single day.

✦

No face, no follow

The majority of her current thumbnails are text on a dark or muted background — no face, no person. At grid size these are completely invisible. Accounts that grow past 100k almost always lead with the creator's face on every thumbnail. People follow people, not quotes.

✦

No visual consistency

Scrolling her grid right now you see pink, olive, black, beige — different fonts, different layouts, different styles on every post. There's no design system tying it together. A strong grid has a signature look so that even at a glance, you know whose content it is.

✦

Text too small to read at grid size

Many of her thumbnails rely on sentences that are only readable when you tap in. On the grid — where the decision to tap is made — the text is too small to register. Thumbnails need one bold statement, large enough to read at the size of a postage stamp.

✦

No curiosity gap

The strongest thumbnails make someone feel like they need to tap to get the answer. The idea is often there in Alicia's current content — but buried in small text on a dark background, it never gets the tap it deserves.

What good looks like

This is the direction every thumbnail should move toward — face visible and expressive, high contrast background, bold readable text at grid size, and a curiosity gap that earns the tap.

Example thumbnail — face-forward, high contrast, readable at grid size

✦

Always lead with Alicia's face

Every thumbnail. Every time. Her expression does half the work — it creates curiosity before anyone reads a word.

✦

One bold statement, large font

Pick the most curiosity-driving phrase and make it the only text. Large enough to read at thumbnail size. A highlight on the key word creates an instant visual anchor.

✦

Build a template and stick to it

Once a thumbnail style is working, turn it into a Canva template. Every video gets the same layout, same fonts, same colour palette — only the text changes.

Interview Interview-style question videos ⌄

A separate short-form format — not clipped from the long-form. Alicia sits in a chair and answers a pointed coaching question asked off-camera. Alex asks during a filming session, or Luke asks casually at home since they live together. The question format lets Alicia answer naturally and thoughtfully, the way she would with a real client. A strong answer often follows a clear structure — "there are usually three reasons..." — which keeps the viewer watching to the end.

Why this format works

It shows Alicia's expertise in real time. A woman watching sees exactly how Alicia thinks through a hard situation — which is the closest thing to experiencing her coaching before booking a call. These are some of the most trust-building videos she can make.

Organic Organic spontaneous content ⌄

Filmed casually on Alicia's phone throughout the week. An everyday moment leads into a personal story, which leads into a marriage lesson — just a moment and a thought, kept simple and conversational. These are much lower effort than the other formats and can be genuine thoughts or stories Alicia wants to share: a memory from years ago, a new experience from that week, or something small that reminded her of a lesson. The one rule is to keep them varied and not repetitive.

These would be nice to have just once or twice a week — but because of the volume and quality of content we're already producing from the long-form and interview videos, these aren't essential. They're a bonus that makes the account feel human, not a requirement. If Alicia has a genuine moment to share, it's welcome. If she doesn't, the week is already covered.

Trust funnel

How content becomes
coaching clients.

Nobody books a coaching call from a stranger. But after weeks of watching someone teach with wisdom, honesty, and heart — they aren't a stranger anymore. This is the journey every piece of content is designed to support.

It's worth being honest about how sensitive this is. Marriage is deeply personal, and many women watching don't yet realize they play a part in the very patterns that are hurting them. That's not a criticism — it's human. So the content can't just hand out advice. It has to gently help a woman see the problem for herself, in a way that feels like relief rather than blame. When someone watches a video and quietly thinks "that's me — I do that," that's the moment real change becomes possible. That recognition is what moves someone from passively watching to genuinely wanting help. The content's job is to create that self-recognition with compassion, so that reaching out for coaching feels like a natural next step toward hope, not an admission of failure.

Product strategy

A course model that
scales without burning out.

The idea is a three-tier product ladder. Women discover Alicia through free content, enter through an affordable niche course, move into the flagship programme when they're ready, and the women who want the most personalised experience invest in private coaching. Each tier serves more women than the one above it — and feeds into it.

"Someone who spends $97 on a niche course and gets a result will come back for the flagship. Someone who goes through the flagship and wants to go even deeper will book private coaching. The content builds the audience. The courses build the relationship. The coaching is the destination."

Tier 1 — Entry

Niche courses.
Specific problems. Lower commitment.

Short, focused courses that target one specific and common marriage challenge. Affordable, always available, no deadline to sign up. These are the entry point for women who aren't ready to commit to the full programme — but who have a specific problem they want help with right now. Each comes with one private call with Alicia.

Tier 3 — Premium

Private 1-on-1 coaching.
For women who want to go all the way.

Private coaching with Alicia stays — but becomes the top of the ladder. Reserved for women who are serious, ready, and want the most personalised experience available. With the course model handling the majority of clients, Alicia can take fewer private clients, give them more focused attention, and charge accordingly.

Current price is $3,000. A small increase — to $3,500 – $4,000 — reflects the premium nature of direct access and is justified by the results Alicia delivers. The boundaries also change: private coaching comes with structured call sessions, not unlimited texting access. This protects Alicia's energy and makes the work more sustainable long-term.

✦

Why the price increase makes sense

As Alicia's content grows and her audience builds, demand for private coaching will increase. Raising the price filters for women who are genuinely committed, reduces the volume of inquiries to a manageable level, and positions Alicia's time at its true value.

✦

Structured access, not unlimited texting

Private coaching should have a defined structure — a set number of calls, clear response time expectations, and a scope of what's included. Women who want to go deeper between sessions can do so through the course material, not through open-ended texting access to Alicia.

✦

The course feeds the coaching

Women who go through the niche courses or the flagship programme and want more will naturally inquire about private coaching. They already trust Alicia, already know the framework, and are the warmest possible leads. The courses aren't competition for the coaching — they're the pipeline.
